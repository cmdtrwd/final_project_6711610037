The Ether

About the Film

Set against the sprawling green rice fields of rural Japan and the flickering glow of early internet chatrooms, All About Lily Chou-Chou (2001) follows the intertwined lives of Yuichi Hasumi and Shusuke Hoshino as they navigate the cruelty and confusion of adolescence.

Director Shunji Iwai crafts a dreamlike portrait of youth caught between connection and isolation, reality and the digital world. The fictional singer Lily Chou-Chou and her ethereal music become a spiritual refuge for Yuichi — a source of the mysterious force fans call the Ether, offering a fragile escape from what he describes as “the Age of Grey.”

Shot on early digital cameras with a raw, overexposed aesthetic, the film interweaves BBS forum posts with scenes of haunting beauty and devastating violence, creating an experience that lingers long after the credits roll.

> I feel like the Ether is the only thing that understands me.
> In this world where everything is grey,
> her voice is the only color I can see.

— Philia @ Lilyholic BBS

Lilyholic

The Community

At the heart of the film lies Lilyholic, an online bulletin board where fans gather to discuss Lily Chou-Chou's music, share their thoughts on the Ether, and find connections they cannot make in the physical world. Under anonymous aliases like Philia and Blue Cat, they exchange intimate confessions hidden behind the safety of the screen.

The BBS messages that overlay much of the film serve as a window into the characters' inner worlds — a stark contrast to their silent suffering in real life. Iwai's pioneering use of internet culture as a narrative device anticipated the age of social media by more than a decade.

> Has anyone been to a Lily concert?
> I heard the Ether is even stronger in person.
> I want to feel it just once before everything ends.

— Blue Cat @ Lilyholic BBS
